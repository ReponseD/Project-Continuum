# Project Continuum Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

Project Continuum is committed to fostering a **safe, inclusive, and welcoming environment** for everyone.  
All participants, including contributors, maintainers, and users, are expected to **respect each other** and help create a community where learning, collaboration, and innovation thrive.  

We pledge to **uphold these principles** in all project spaces, whether online or offline.

---

## Our Standards

Participants are expected to:

- **Be welcoming and inclusive**: Use language and behavior that invite participation from all people, regardless of identity or background.  
- **Be respectful**: Listen to others, avoid personal attacks, and disagree constructively.  
- **Give constructive feedback**: Focus on ideas and solutions, not individuals.  
- **Be collaborative**: Work together to improve the project and community.  
- **Be mindful of impact**: Consider how your words and actions affect others.  

Unacceptable behaviors include, but are not limited to:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form.  
- Offensive or derogatory comments related to personal characteristics (e.g., age, gender, race, ethnicity, religion, sexual orientation, disability, or appearance).  
- Publishing private information (doxxing) without explicit consent.  
- Threatening or violent behavior.  
- Sustained disruption of discussions, code reviews, or events.  
- Inappropriate or offensive content in code, documentation, comments, or media.  

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies **to all project spaces** and activities, including:

- GitHub repositories, pull requests, and issues.  
- Community forums, Slack/Discord channels, and social media related to Project Continuum.  
- Workshops, hackathons, competitions, and other in-person or virtual events.  
- Any public interactions representing Project Continuum.  

---

## Reporting Incidents

If you witness or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

1. **Report it immediately** via email: **kaleeba.isma@gmail.com**  
2. Include relevant details:  
   - What happened  
   - Who was involved  
   - When and where it occurred  
   - Any evidence (screenshots, messages, links)  

All reports will be treated **confidentially** and reviewed promptly.  

---

## Enforcement

Project Continuum maintainers are responsible for enforcing this Code of Conduct.  
Consequences for violations may include:

- **Verbal or written warnings**  
- **Temporary or permanent removal** from project spaces  
- **Revocation of contributor privileges**  
- **Reporting to authorities** if legal action is required  

Maintainers reserve the right to **take immediate action** to protect community safety. Enforcement decisions are final and intended to maintain a healthy, respectful environment.

---

## Responsibility of Maintainers

Maintainers must:

- Clarify and communicate standards of acceptable behavior.  
- Monitor community interactions for compliance.  
- Respond promptly to reports of misconduct.  
- Treat all complaints with fairness and confidentiality.  
- Lead by example in promoting inclusive and respectful behavior.  

---

## Community Commitment

By participating in Project Continuum, you agree to:

- Uphold this Code of Conduct at all times.  
- Treat others with respect and professionalism.  
- Help foster a collaborative, safe, and positive environment.
